I am thrilled to be (virtually) in Austin today for the Texas Department of Information Resources (DIR) **World Usability Day** celebration!

It was a true honor to step in for my colleague [Beatriz González Mellídez](https://bsky.app/profile/batish.bsky.social) today and join [James Thurston](https://bsky.app/profile/jamesthu.bsky.social) on stage to discuss one of the most pressing challenges in our industry: **Training Tech Teams for Inclusive AI and Better Accessibility**.

## Real-World Training for Real-World Impact
Inclusive AI isn't just a buzzword; it’s a design imperative. In our session, James and I dove deep into the practicalities of how we bridge the gap between "technical capability" and "human-centered design." Drawing from our hands-on experience in the field, we explored:

- **Mindset over Metadata:** How to shift a development team’s focus from mere compliance to genuine empathy.
- **The "Inclusive AI" Framework:** Strategies for training developers to spot bias and leverage AI to break down accessibility barriers rather than build new ones.
- **Practical Workflows:** Sharing the specific training modules we’ve used to help technical teams integrate accessibility into their daily sprints.
